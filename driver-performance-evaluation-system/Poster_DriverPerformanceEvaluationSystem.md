## Abstract
Data-Driven Decision Making is vital to achieve strategic objectives in current industrial activities. The project assesses a Last-Mile company's performance system through Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) and Data Science methodologies. It succeeded in ranking a series of drivers through multi-dimensional data and Machine Learning approaches. The obtained insights are valuable to identify gaps and areas of improvement, fostering a continuous improvement culture within the company.

---

## Introduction
Currently, NextGEN lacks an efficient way to define accurately the performance of its drivers. Without a concise performance system, it becomes difficult to make informed decisions and implement improvements on its services. 

The challenge lies in assessing and developing the planning and control processes at NextGen Deliveries. Our proposed approach targets the organization's system of performance indicators to identify gaps and areas for improvement.

---

## Methodology
At first hand, the project utilized Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis to propose a global score for the ranking of drivers. The **Analytical Hierarchical Process (AHP)** was applied to obtain weights for the 18 available individual KPIs.

* **Pairwise Comparisons:** Pairwise quantifiable comparisons between Criteria and Sub-criteria allowed the analysis to obtain standardized weights for each one of the 18 variables.
* **Alignment:** The results align directly with the qualitative analysis developed during consultation sessions with the training partner.
* **Global Scoring:** These weights enabled the calculation of a global score for each one of the 52 drivers included in the analysis.
* **Ranking:** Ranking them in descending order allows us to compare and accurately define the performance of NextGEN's drivers. 

Subsequently, **Cluster Analysis** was used to gather statistical insights regarding the proposed ranking.

### 2. Insights through Cluster Analysis
Drivers were segmented into three specific groups after ranking to understand operational variances:
* **Cluster 1:** High overall metrics, but needs specific focus to improve in customer engagement.
* **Cluster 2:** Mid-tier performance, with primary recommendations to improve delivery documentation processes.
* **Cluster 3:** Lower-tier performance group, exhibiting clear operational gaps; strategic target is to improve inefficiencies in delivery processes.

A spider diagram was utilized to portray and validate the functionality of the ranking across three control baselines: the Best Driver (1st), Median Driver (26th), and Worst Driver (52nd).

The project successfully achieved its objective of analyzing and developing a new performance ranking system using specified Multi-Criteria Decision Making and Data Science methodologies. Through Data-Driven Decision Making, the system successfully supports strategic logistics objectives alongside statistical, real-life insights.
